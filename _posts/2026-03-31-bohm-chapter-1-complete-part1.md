## 第一部分：引言與問題定義（第1-8頁）

### 段落 1.1（第1頁）

#### 英文原文
> The title of this chapter is 'Fragmentation and wholeness'. It is especially important to consider this question today, for fragmentation is now very widespread, not only throughout society, but also in each individual; and this is leading to a kind of general confusion of the mind, which creates an endless series of problems and interferes with our clarity of perception so seriously as to prevent us from being able to solve most of them.

#### 中文翻譯
> 本章的標題是「碎片化與整體性」。今天特別需要考慮這個問題，因為碎片化現在非常普遍，不僅遍及整個社會，也存在於每個個體之中；這導致了一種普遍的心智混亂，產生了一系列無窮無盡的問題，嚴重干擾了我們的感知清晰度，以至於我們無法解決大多數問題。

**頁碼參考**：第1頁

---

### 段落 1.2（第1-2頁）

#### 英文原文
> Thus art, science, technology, and human work in general, are divided up into specialities, each considered to be separate in essence from the others. Becoming dissatisfied with this state of affairs, men have set up further interdisciplinary subjects, which were intended to unite these specialities, but these new subjects have ultimately served mainly to add further separate fragments.

#### 中文翻譯
> 因此，藝術、科學、技術以及一般的人類工作被劃分為各個專業領域，每個領域在本質上都被認為是彼此分離的。人們對這種狀況感到不滿，於是建立了更多的跨學科科目，旨在統一這些專業領域，但這些新科目最終主要只是增加了更多分離的碎片。

**頁碼參考**：第1-2頁

---

### 段落 1.3（第2頁）

#### 英文原文
> Then, society as a whole has developed in such a way that it is broken up into separate nations and different religious, political, economic, racial groups, etc. Man's natural environment has correspondingly been seen as an aggregate of separately existent parts, to be exploited by different groups of people.

#### 中文翻譯
> 然後，整個社會的發展方式使其分裂為不同的國家以及不同的宗教、政治、經濟、種族群體等。相應地，人類的自然環境被視為獨立存在部分的集合，由不同群體的人們進行開發利用。

**頁碼參考**：第2頁

---

### 段落 1.4（第2-3頁）

#### 英文原文
> Similarly, each individual human being has been fragmented into a large number of separate and conflicting compartments, according to his different desires, aims, ambitions, loyalties, psychological characteristics, etc., to such an extent that it is generally accepted that some degree of neurosis is inevitable, while many individuals going beyond the 'normal' limits of fragmentation are classified as paranoid, schizoid, psychotic, etc.

#### 中文翻譯
> 同樣地，每個個體的人類也被碎片化為大量分離且衝突的隔間，根據其不同的慾望、目標、抱負、忠誠、心理特徵等，以至於人們普遍接受某種程度的神經症是不可避免的，而許多超出碎片化「正常」限度的個體被歸類為偏執狂、分裂型、精神病等。

**頁碼參考**：第2-3頁

---

### 段落 1.5（第3頁）

#### 英文原文
> The notion that all these fragments are separately existent is evidently an illusion, and this illusion cannot do other than lead to endless conflict and confusion. Indeed, the attempt to live according to the notion that the fragments are really separate is, in essence, what has led to the growing series of extremely urgent crises that is confronting us today.

#### 中文翻譯
> 認為所有這些碎片都是獨立存在的觀念顯然是一種幻覺，而這種幻覺除了導致無盡的衝突和混亂之外，別無他果。事實上，試圖按照碎片真正分離的觀念生活，本質上就是導致我們今天面臨的一系列日益緊迫危機的原因。

**頁碼參考**：第3頁

---

### 段落 1.6（第3-4頁）

#### 英文原文
> Thus, as is now well known, this way of life has brought about pollution, destruction of the balance of nature, over-population, world-wide economic and political disorder, and the creation of an overall environment that is neither physically nor mentally healthy for most of the people who have to live in it.

#### 中文翻譯
> 因此，正如現在眾所周知的，這種生活方式導致了污染、自然平衡的破壞、人口過剩、全球經濟和政治混亂，以及創造了一個對大多數必須生活在其中的人來說既不身體健康也不心理健康的整體環境。

**頁碼參考**：第3-4頁

---

### 段落 1.7（第4頁）

#### 英文原文
> Individually there has developed a widespread feeling of helplessness and despair, in the face of what seems to be an overwhelming mass of disparate social forces, going beyond the control and even the comprehension of the human beings who are caught up in it.

#### 中文翻譯
> 在個人層面，面對看似壓倒性的、不相關的社會力量，人們普遍感到無助和絕望，這些力量超出了陷入其中的人類的控制甚至理解能力。

**頁碼參考**：第4頁

---

### 段落 1.8（第4-5頁）

#### 英文原文
> Indeed, to some extent, it has always been both necessary and proper for man, in his thinking, to divide things up, and to separate them, so as to reduce his problems to manageable proportions; for evidently, if in our practical technical work we tried to deal with the whole of reality all at once, we would be lost in a confusion of irrelevant details.

#### 中文翻譯
> 事實上，在某種程度上，人類在思考時將事物劃分開來、分離它們，既是必要的也是恰當的，以便將問題縮小到可管理的範圍；因為顯然，如果我們在實際技術工作中試圖一次性處理整個現實，我們將迷失在不相關細節的混亂中。

**頁碼參考**：第4-5頁

---

### 段落 1.9（第5頁）

#### 英文原文
> Nevertheless, this way of thinking has become so habitual and pervasive that we are generally not aware of it, and we therefore fail to notice that our thought is continually dividing things into separate entities, which are considered to be essentially independent and self-existent.

#### 中文翻譯
> 然而，這種思考方式已經變得如此習慣性和普遍，以至於我們通常意識不到它，因此我們未能注意到我們的思想不斷地將事物劃分為獨立的實體，這些實體被認為在本質上是獨立和自我存在的。

**頁碼參考**：第5頁

---

### 段落 1.10（第5-6頁）

#### 英文原文
> The point that is being made here is that this fragmentary way of thinking is leading to a fragmentary way of acting, which in turn is leading to the various difficulties that have been mentioned. What is needed is a way of thinking that is aware of the limits of the fragmentary approach, and that is capable of seeing things as whole, even while it is necessary, for practical purposes, to give some attention to the parts.

#### 中文翻譯
> 這裡要指出的觀點是，這種碎片化的思考方式導致了碎片化的行動方式，這反過來又導致了前面提到的各種困難。我們需要的是一種能夠意識到碎片化方法局限性的思考方式，一種即使出於實際目的需要關注部分時，也能夠將事物視為整體的思考方式。

**頁碼參考**：第5-6頁

---

### 段落 1.11（第6頁）

#### 英文原文
> Such a way of thinking would not deny the value of analysis and specialization, but it would see them as limited tools, to be used with caution, and always within the context of a broader understanding of the whole.

#### 中文翻譯
> 這樣的思考方式不會否認分析和專業化的價值，但會將它們視為有限的工具，需要謹慎使用，並且始終處於對整體的更廣泛理解的背景中。

**頁碼參考**：第6頁

---

### 段落 1.12（第6-7頁）

#### 英文原文
> The development of such a way of thinking is not merely a matter of intellectual understanding. It requires a fundamental change in our whole approach to life, a change that involves not only our thinking, but also our feeling, our will, and our action.

#### 中文翻譯
> 發展這樣的思考方式不僅僅是智力理解的問題。它需要我們對生活的整個態度發生根本性變化，這種變化不僅涉及我們的思考，還涉及我們的感受、意志和行動。

**頁碼參考**：第6-7頁

---

### 段落 1.13（第7頁）

#### 英文原文
> In this chapter, we shall explore the nature of fragmentation and wholeness, and consider how a more holistic approach might be developed. We shall begin by examining the historical roots of our fragmentary way of thinking, and then go on to consider its manifestations in various fields of human activity.

#### 中文翻譯
> 在本章中，我們將探討碎片化和整體性的本質，並考慮如何發展更整體的方法。我們將首先檢視我們碎片化思考方式的歷史根源，然後繼續考慮它在人類活動各個領域中的表現。

**頁碼參考**：第7頁

---

### 段落 1.14（第7-8頁）

#### 英文原文
> Finally, we shall discuss the possibility of a new kind of science, a new kind of society, and a new kind of individual, all based on a more holistic understanding of reality. This discussion will lead naturally into the later chapters of the book, where these ideas will be developed in more detail.

#### 中文翻譯
> 最後，我們將討論一種新型科學、新型社會和新型個體的可能性，這些都基於對現實的更整體理解。這個討論將自然地引向本書的後續章節，在那裡這些想法將得到更詳細的發展。

**頁碼參考**：第7-8頁
